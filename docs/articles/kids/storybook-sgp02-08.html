🪂 Emergency Plans

How Robots Stay Safe When Things Go Wrong

A story of parachutes, fallback plans, and why every robot has a way home

⚠️ What If Things Go Wrong?

Robots live in harsh places: factory floors, oil rigs, moving vehicles. Power can cut out mid-download. The Commander's server could crash. A profile could be corrupted. SGP.02 knew this, so it built in emergency plans that protect the robot no matter what happens.

🔁 The Fallback Plan

Every robot can hold multiple profiles. The Fallback Plan is simple: keep one profile on the primary network and another on a backup network. If the primary fails, the Commander simply sends ENABLE on the backup profile. The robot switches instantly: no new download needed!

🚨 Emergency + 🧪 Test Missions

SGP.02 has emergency ISD-P rooms sitting empty, ready for urgent profiles. The Fallback Mechanism can instantly revert to the old profile if a new one fails. And before rolling out to 10,000 robots, the Commander always does a test mission on just one robot first: safe and smart!

🔧 Local Control

Even if the Commander is completely unreachable, a technician with a physical toolkit can connect directly to the robot's ISD-R: its private communications channel. Through ISD-R, they can enable, disable, or diagnose profiles locally. No network needed. It's the ultimate emergency plan!

The ISD-R channel is the secret to SGP.02 resilience. Even if over-the-air commands fail, ISD-R provides a direct physical link to the robot's secure environment. It's like having an emergency door that opens with a physical key: no network, no passwords, just pure hardware security!
